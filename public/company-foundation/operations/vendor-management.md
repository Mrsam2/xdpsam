# Vendor Management

## Purpose

Manage third-party vendors and suppliers to control cost, risk, and dependency.

## Vendor lifecycle

1. **Need & selection.** Define the requirement; evaluate options on fit, cost, reliability, and security.
2. **Due diligence.** For vendors touching our data: review their security and privacy posture; sign a DPA if personal data is involved.
3. **Contracting.** Clear terms: scope, price, SLAs, data handling, term, and exit. Founder approval per spend matrix.
4. **Onboarding.** Least-privilege access; document the owner.
5. **Ongoing review.** Track performance, cost, and renewal dates; review periodically.
6. **Offboarding.** Revoke access, retrieve/delete data, confirm contract closure.

## Vendor register

Maintain a register with: vendor name, service, internal owner, cost, renewal date, data accessed, and risk level.

## Risk principles

- Avoid critical single points of failure where feasible; know your contingency.
- Higher data sensitivity = higher due-diligence bar.
- Review concentration: don't become dangerously dependent on one vendor.
