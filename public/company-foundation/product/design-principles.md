# Design Principles

## Our design beliefs

- **Clarity over cleverness.** Users should never have to think about how to use it.
- **Consistency.** Same patterns, same behavior everywhere. Build and reuse a design system.
- **Accessible by default.** Meet recognized accessibility standards (e.g., WCAG AA). Accessibility is a requirement, not a feature.
- **Fast is a feature.** Performance and responsiveness are part of UX.
- **Trustworthy.** Clear copy, honest states, no dark patterns.

## Design process

1. Understand the user and their job-to-be-done.
2. Sketch low-fidelity flows; validate the concept cheaply.
3. Design high-fidelity using the design system.
4. Test with real users; iterate.
5. Hand off with clear specs; collaborate with engineering through delivery.

## Design system

- Maintain reusable components, tokens (color, type, spacing), and patterns.
- Document usage so engineers and designers stay aligned.
- Evolve it deliberately; changes are reviewed like code.

## Quality bar

- Responsive across target devices.
- Empty, loading, error, and success states designed, not afterthoughts.
- Content/UX writing is clear, concise, and consistent with brand voice.
