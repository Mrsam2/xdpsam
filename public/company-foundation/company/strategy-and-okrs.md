# Strategy & OKRs

## Strategic horizons

We plan across three horizons so we balance survival today with ambition tomorrow.

- **Horizon 1 (0–12 months):** Find product-market fit in one beachhead market. Generate first revenue. Build the founding team.
- **Horizon 2 (1–3 years):** Scale the proven offering. Establish repeatable sales and delivery. Expand into 1–2 adjacent verticals.
- **Horizon 3 (3–5 years):** Platform and product lines across multiple industries. Recognized brand. Durable margins.

## Beachhead strategy

We do **not** chase every industry at once, even though the XDP's brand can span many. We pick one beachhead where we have an unfair advantage, win it, then expand. Default sequencing:

1. **Services / consulting** (Cloud, DevOps, AI enablement) to generate early cash and learn customer problems.
2. **Productized services** (repeatable packages) to improve margins.
3. **SaaS products** built from patterns we see repeatedly across clients.

## OKR framework

We run **quarterly OKRs**, cascaded from company to team to individual.

- **Objectives** are qualitative, ambitious, and time-boxed.
- **Key Results** are measurable outcomes (not tasks). 3–5 per objective.
- Scoring 0.0–1.0; 0.7 is a healthy stretch target.

### OKR cadence

| When | Activity |
|---|---|
| 2 weeks before quarter | Draft company OKRs |
| Quarter start | Finalize and publish all OKRs |
| Weekly | Team check-in on KR progress |
| Mid-quarter | Confidence review, re-forecast |
| Quarter end | Score, retro, archive |

### Example company OKR (Horizon 1)

**Objective:** Prove we can win and delight our first paying customers.

- **KR1:** Sign 3 paying clients with total contracted revenue ≥ target.
- **KR2:** Achieve ≥ 9/10 average client satisfaction (CSAT) on first deliveries.
- **KR3:** Convert ≥ 1 client into a recurring/retainer relationship.

All OKRs are tracked as GitLab issues/epics linked to this document.
