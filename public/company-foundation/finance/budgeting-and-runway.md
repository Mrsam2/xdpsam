# Budgeting & Runway

## Runway is the master metric

**Runway = cash on hand ÷ net monthly burn.** Everything else is secondary if runway hits zero. We always know our runway.

## Budgeting approach

- Build an annual budget, reviewed quarterly, with monthly forecasts.
- **Zero-based mindset early:** justify spend rather than rolling forward.
- Separate **fixed** (salaries, tools, rent) from **variable** (project, marketing) costs.
- Tie major spend to OKRs and expected return.

## Burn discipline

- Track gross burn (total spend) and net burn (spend minus revenue).
- Categorize spend; review the biggest line items regularly.
- Before any new recurring cost, ask: does this extend or shorten runway, and is it worth it?

## Scenario planning

Maintain three scenarios and the trigger points between them:

| Scenario | Trigger | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Base** | On plan | Execute roadmap |
| **Upside** | Revenue ahead of plan | Reinvest deliberately in growth |
| **Downside** | Revenue/runway below plan | Cut discretionary spend; protect core; extend runway |

## Runway thresholds (set explicitly)

- **Healthy:** > 12 months — invest in growth.
- **Caution:** 6–12 months — tighten spend, accelerate revenue/fundraise.
- **Critical:** < 6 months — emergency plan: cut burn, prioritize cash-collecting work.
