# Fundraising

> Optional, not mandatory. We can build a strong business on revenue (bootstrapping). Raise only when capital clearly accelerates a validated opportunity.

## Bootstrapped vs. funded

- **Default: bootstrap** via services revenue to retain control and force discipline.
- **Raise** when there's a proven, capital-efficient growth lever and the upside justifies dilution.

## Funding stages (if we pursue investment)

| Stage | Purpose | What investors expect |
|---|---|---|
| **Pre-seed / Angel** | Build MVP, early traction | Strong team, clear problem |
| **Seed** | Find product-market fit | Early traction, retention signals |
| **Series A+** | Scale proven model | Repeatable growth, unit economics |

## Readiness checklist

- [ ] Clean cap table (`governance/equity-and-cap-table.md`).
- [ ] Up-to-date financials and metrics.
- [ ] Clear story: problem, solution, market, traction, team, ask, use of funds.
- [ ] Data room: incorporation docs, contracts, financials, IP, cap table.
- [ ] Defined raise amount, valuation expectations, and milestones the round buys.

## Process discipline

- Run fundraising as a tight, time-boxed process to minimize distraction.
- Talk to many investors in parallel to create momentum.
- Founders make all fundraising decisions (Tier 1) with legal review of every term sheet.
- Understand dilution and control implications before signing anything.
