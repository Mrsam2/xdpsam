# Culture & Remote Work

## Operating model

We default to **remote-first and async-first**. We optimize for outcomes, not hours or location.

## Async-first principles

- **Write things down.** Decisions, context, and updates live in GitLab/docs, not in someone's head.
- **Default to public** channels over DMs so context is shared.
- **Respect time zones.** No expectation of instant replies. Use clear deadlines.
- **Meetings have a purpose, agenda, and notes** or they don't happen.

## Communication norms

| Channel | Use for |
|---|---|
| GitLab issues/MRs | Work, decisions, reviews, tasks |
| Docs (this repo) | Durable policy and process |
| Chat | Quick coordination, not decisions of record |
| Video calls | Relationship-building, complex/sensitive topics |

## Rituals that keep us connected

- Weekly team sync (short, focused).
- Async weekly update from each person (wins, blockers, next).
- Periodic virtual social time.
- Quarterly retros to improve how we work.

## Working hours & wellbeing

- Define core overlap hours per team for collaboration; flexible otherwise.
- Sustainable pace is a value, not a perk. No glorifying burnout.
- Take your time off. Managers model healthy boundaries.
