# Data Protection & Privacy

> We treat personal and customer data with care and respect, following recognized privacy principles (GDPR-style) by default, regardless of where we operate.

## Privacy principles

1. **Lawfulness & transparency:** collect data with a clear basis and tell people how it's used.
2. **Purpose limitation:** use data only for stated purposes.
3. **Data minimization:** collect only what you need.
4. **Accuracy:** keep data correct and current.
5. **Storage limitation:** keep data only as long as needed.
6. **Integrity & confidentiality:** secure it.
7. **Accountability:** be able to demonstrate compliance.

## Data classification

| Class | Examples | Handling |
|---|---|---|
| **Public** | Marketing content | No restriction |
| **Internal** | Internal docs, plans | Employees only |
| **Confidential** | Contracts, financials, source code | Need-to-know, encrypted |
| **Customer/Personal** | PII, customer data | Strictest controls, DPA, encryption |

## Data subject rights

Be able to honor requests to access, correct, delete, or export personal data within legally required timeframes.

## Required documents

- **Privacy Policy** (public, on website).
- **Data Processing Agreements (DPAs)** with customers and sub-processors.
- **Records of processing activities** (what data, why, where, how long).

## Practical rules

- Never share customer data without authorization and a lawful basis.
- Encrypt personal data; restrict access to need-to-know.
- Choose vendors/sub-processors that meet our privacy standards.
- Breaches involving personal data follow `security/incident-response-plan.md` and any legal notification duties.
